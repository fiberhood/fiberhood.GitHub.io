Species: Geranium phaeum 

Family: Geraniaceae  
Genus:  Geranium
Species description: Perennial, with short, stout, oblique rhizome. Stems 40-70 cm, erect, with short glandular and long, patent eglandular hairs. Leaves 6-15 cm wide, divided for c. 70% of radius into (5-)7 broadly oblong, weakly pinnatifid lobes.Inflorescence axillary or leaf-opposed, and also terminal, rather lax, many flowered;pedicels remaining erect after flowering.Sepals 8-9 mm, with aresta c. 1 mm, densely glandular puberulent and also with long egalndular hairs.Petals 8-10 x 6-10 mm, obovate-orbicular, entire, erose or apiculate, blackish or brownish-purple or dull lilac.Mericarps hirsute, with 2-4 strong transverse ridges below the style

Field Notes:

Habitat: Damp or shady places

Distribution: C.Europe, extended to Pyrenees$ C.Italy, Bulgaria and tge W.borders of U.S.S.R. Also as an escape from gardens. Al Au Bu Co Cz Ga Ge He Hs Hu It Ju Po Rm Rs(C, W) [Be Br Da Hb Ho Su]
