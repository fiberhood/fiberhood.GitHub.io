Species: Drosophyllum lusitanica 

Family: Droseraceae  
Genus:  Drosophyllum
Species description: Stem slender, ascending, sometimes brached. Leaves 10-20 x 2-3 cm, numerous, alternate, crowded, narrowly linear, tapering from the sheating base tothe filiform apex, covered throughout with red-tipped, glandular, digestive hairs. Scape 15-30 mm, erect, bearing a corymb of 5-10 flowers. Petals 18-25 mm, broadly obovate, bright yellow.

Field Notes:

Habitat: Dry places; calcifuge

Distribution: S.Spain and Portugal. Hs Lu.

Found: S. Spain: S de Ronda: Right-side of road from Ubrique to Alcala de los Gazules: 12.8 km from cross-road to Cortes de la Frontera.

Date: 5-93; 7-5-94

Flora Europea:1-350

Literatuur:

Notes: Often together with Erica Umbellata, which is also calcifuge.
