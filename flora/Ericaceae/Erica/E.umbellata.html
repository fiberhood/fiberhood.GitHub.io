Species: Erica umbellata 

Family: Ericaceae  
Genus:  Erica
Species description: Dwarf shrub 20-80 cm; young twigs pubescent. Leaves 3-4 mm, in worls of 3, linear, suberect, more or less pubescent or glandular-ciliate when young, later glabrous; margin revolute, contiguous, completely concealing the lower surface. Flowers in terminal umbels of 3-6; pedicels 2-4 mm, pubescent, with 2-3 bracteoles immidiately below the sepals. Sepals 2 mm, ovate, shortly ciliate; corolla 3.5-5.5 mm, bright pinkish-putple, subglobose to broadly campanulate, with erect lobes; anthers usually excerted, without appendages . Ovary glabrous; stigma obconical.

Plants generally similar to E.umbellata, but with pedicels up to 7 mm, corolla 6-6.5 mm, and anthers abortive and included, occur here and there throughout its range. They may be hybrids, but the identity of the other parent is obscure.

Field Notes:

Habitat: Heaths, scrub and open woods; calcifuge

Distribution: W. half of the Iberian peninsula. Hs Lu

Found: S.Spain: S de Ronda
