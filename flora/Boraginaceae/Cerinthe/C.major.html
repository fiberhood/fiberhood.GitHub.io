Species: Cerinthe major 

Family: Boraginaceae  
Genus:  Cerinthe
Species description: Annuals; stems 15-60 cm. Lower leaves obovate-spathulate, petiolate, ciliate, tuberculate; the upper ovate-lanceolate, cordate at base. Bracts ovate, subcordate at base, often redish-glaucous, equalling or longer than calyx. Calyx-lobes acute, ciliate. Corolla 15-30 x 5-8 mm, slightly saccate, straight, yellow, with a reddish-brown ring in the throat, sometimes cream distally, or dark red throughout, more than twice as long as calyx; lobes ovate, sharply recurved at apex. Nutlets blackish.

Field Notes:

Habitat: Cultivated fields, Waste places and meadows

Distribution: Med.region, C.& S.Portugal. Al Co Cr Ga Gr Hs It Lu Sa Si Tu

Found: S.Spain: Barbate de Franco
