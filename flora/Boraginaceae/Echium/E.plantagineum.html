Species: Echium plantagineum 

Family: Boraginaceae  
Genus:  Echium
Species description: Erect, softly hairy, annual or biennal 20-60 cm, with 1 t0 many flowerind stems.Basal leaves 50-140 x 15(25) mm, ovate, with prominent lateral veins and soft apressed setea; culaine, cauine leaves oblong to lanceolate,, hte uppermost more or less cordate at the base.Inflorescens usually branched.Calyx 7-10 mm at anthesis, up to 15 mm in fruit.Corolla 18-30 mm, infundibuliform, blue becoming pink through purple, hairy on veins and margins only, usualy with 2 exerted stamens.

Field Notes:

Habitat: Roadsides, fields and sandy areas near the sea

Distribution: S.& W. Europe, northwards to S.W.England; occusually elsewhere Al Az Bl Br Co Cr Ga Gr Hs It Ju Lu Rs(C,K) Sa Si Tu

Found: Spain: E.Sierra Morena
