Species: Viola cazorlensis 

Family: Violaceae  
Genus:  Viola
Species description: Stems 5-10 cm, numerous, crowded, erect. Leaves 0.75-1.5 cm, linear to lanceolate, acute, entire, sessile. Stipules slightly shorter than leaves. Flowers intense pinkish-purple, on long penduncles. Sepals acute. Petals narrow, the lower emarginate. Spur 20-30 mm, equaling or exceeding petals Capsules glabrous)

Field Notes: 1996: verry abundant at some places.

Habitat: Limestone crevices in mountains, 1000-2000 m.

Distribution: Spain (prov.Jaen) Hs

Found: S.E.Spain: Sierra de Cazorla

Date: 15-7-1979/ 1996

Flora Europea:2-282

Literatuur:

Notes: Fl. of S.W.Europe, page 86: Tertaire relict, closely related to V.Kosanninii in N.Albania, N.Makedonia and V.delphinantha .N.greece and Bulgaria
