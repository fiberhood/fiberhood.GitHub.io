Species: Althea officinalis 

Family: Malvaceae  
Genus:  Althea
Species description: Densely grey-pubescent perennial up to 200 cm; allhairs stellate. Leaves triangular-ovate, acute, crenate serrate, undivided or palmately lobed, often somewhat plicate. Flowers solitary or clustered in axillary and terminal inflorescences; peduncle of axillary inflorescenses shorter than the subtending leaf. Epicalyx-segments linear-lanceolate. Sepals ovate, acute, curved over the fruit. Petals 15-20 cm, very pale lilac-pink, rarely deeper pink. Anthers purplish-red. Mericarps more or less densely covered with stellate hairs, smooth.

Habitat: Damp places.
